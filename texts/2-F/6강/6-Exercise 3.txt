**Emotions** play a **gatekeeper** role in **cognition**. The **allocation** of **attention** and working memory does not happen by miracle. We **solve** **problems** that are only **emotionally** **important** to us. Think of emotions as a **biological** **thermostat** that **activates** attention, which then activates problem-solving and response systems. When danger or opportunity arises, **information** from our **senses** triggers an **emotional** **reaction** that **informs** the **brain** that something requires further attention and maybe some **problem** **solving**. Emotions **operate** **unconsciously**, evaluating sensory information from our total environment, even while we're asleep or attending to other things. When our emotions enter **awareness**, we call it a feeling, and then we can begin to **consciously** deal with the challenge using our **reasoning** abilities.
