**Information** **technology** has great **potential** for **influencing** markets. It **bridges** time and distance in a completely **new** way and opens markets previously **unattainable** because they were geographically too **remote**. Until recently, a **small** **town** would have no more than three **banks** **competing** with each other for the **business** of the town's **residents**. Today, literally **thousands** of national and international banks compete for **customers** in that same small town. Financial institutions no longer have to maintain a **physical** **presence** to do business. Financial **transactions**, such as **buying** and **selling** shares, are **executed** **electronically**, and the customer can **monitor** such transactions on his or her personal **Internet** page. In 2000, more than half of all stock orders placed by individuals in the United States were initiated via the Internet.
