**Emotions** play a **gatekeeper** role in **cognition**. The **allocation** of **attention** and working memory does not happen by miracle. We **attempt** to solve problems that are only **emotionally** **important** to us. Think of emotions as a **biological** **thermostat** that **activates** attention, which then activates a rich set of problem solving and **response** systems. When danger or **opportunity** arises, information from our senses **triggers** an emotional **reaction** that **informs** the rest of the brain that something requires further attention and maybe even some problem solving. Emotions operate **unconsciously** **constantly** **evaluating** sensory information from our total environment, even while we're asleep or attending to other things. When our emotions enter **awareness**, we call it a feeling, and then we can begin to **consciously** deal with the challenge using our **reasoning** **abilities**.
